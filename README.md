Global Sales Profitability & Supply Chain Margin Optimization Analysis
​Executive Summary
​This advanced financial data analytics project focuses on dissecting corporate profitability structures and regional logistics supply-chain efficiencies across key global hubs (Dubai, London, New York). Utilizing Advanced Excel Pivot Modeling and Calculated Fields, I developed custom financial metrics to expose hidden cost drivers and isolate true operating profitability from top-line revenue illusions.
​Technical Stack & Applied Frameworks
​Tool: Microsoft Excel (Advanced Pivot Tables, Fields Modeling)
​Advanced Formula Calculations: Embedded custom Calculated Fields directly within the data architecture.
​Financial Architecture: Multi-factor cost allocation, landed cost benchmark modeling, and executive margin analysis.

C-Suite Executive Interpretation & Risk Assessment
​The Revenue Illusion Effect: The New York branch drives a massive transaction volume, yet converts it at the lowest net operational efficiency (27.11% Net Margin) compared to Dubai's stellar 47.16% efficiency. This signals that massive sales volume is actively masking fundamental cost stress.
​Critical Supply-Chain Friction: Logistics costs (Freight + Customs) in New York have escalated to an alarming 69.95% of the core manufacturing value (COGS), and 56.25% in London. This extreme overhead absorbs operating margins and represents a non-sustainable bottleneck requiring urgent structural realignment.
​Strategic Advisory Roadmap
​Structural Freight Overhaul: Initiate immediate competitive bidding and long-term volume contract renegotiations for the transatlantic freight lanes to reduce the 69.95% logistics cost ratio.
​Tariff & Duty Engineering: Conduct an exhaustive customs audit at the New York port of entry. Reclassify premium textile components under more favorable HS codes to legally minimize localized customs exposure.
​Data-Driven Capital Allocation: Direct immediate corporate expansion capital toward the Dubai hub to maximize high-efficiency cash conversion (47.16% margin) while western supply-chain structures undergo mitigation.
